The challenge

For the U.S. AgTech founder at Seed to Series B
01 · The ceiling
You are the pipeline, and that is the problem
Founder-led sales works until it doesn't. In AgTech, where sales cycles are long and buyers are relationship-driven, growth capped at your personal bandwidth means deals stall the moment you stop pushing.
There is no process to hand over. No automated outbound running while you are at a field demo. No system getting smarter between conversations. The transition from founder-led to founder-informed sales requires a sales engine: lead generation, automated outreach, and continuous pitch improvement, not just a playbook. That engine is what Reveneering builds and leaves with you.
"The issue isn't with founding activity. Early-stage participation is strong. But sustaining momentum is where the sector falls short."
AgFunder Network Partners, U.S. AgTech Investment Report, 2025
02 · The buyer
AgTech buyers don't buy like SaaS buyers
Whether you are selling to a food company, a retailer, a cooperative, or a government agency, the procurement logic is different. Relationship, risk tolerance, and proof of concept come before price.
A playbook written for enterprise SaaS will stall at the first procurement conversation with a food manufacturer or a USDA-adjacent buyer. Reveneering's approach is built around how agricultural buyers actually make decisions: field validation before contract, long approval chains, and procurement criteria tied to operational risk, not feature sets.
"Adoption barriers in agriculture are as much about trust and proof as they are about price. Farmers and food companies need to see it work before they commit."
Purdue Center for Food and Agricultural Business, 2024
03 · The gap
Strong technology. Weak commercial infrastructure
U.S. AgTech has exceptional product depth. The gap is almost always commercial: the distance between a successful pilot and a signed enterprise contract, or between a grant and a paying customer.
Most AgTech founders can demonstrate ROI in the field. Far fewer have a documented sales process that converts that demonstration into a repeatable commercial motion. Reveneering exists to close that gap: building the lead generation, outbound, and pitch infrastructure that turns strong technology into consistent revenue.
"AgTech investment has matured significantly, but commercial execution remains the primary bottleneck between promising pilots and scalable revenue."
AgFunder U.S. AgriFood Tech Investment Report, 2025
04 · The clock
Your investors are waiting for revenue that matches the story
AgTech fundraising is tightening. Investors who once backed technology potential now want commercial traction. A pipeline that exists in your head is not a commercial model.
Every quarter without a documented sales system makes the next raise harder. Reveneering's 90-day sprint ends with a board-ready pipeline, a revenue forecast, and a running engine your investors can evaluate. Not a deck of intentions. A live commercial operation.
"Investors are increasingly focused on near-term revenue potential and commercial validation rather than long-term technology bets."
PitchBook AgTech Market Report, Q1 2025

Experience directly relevant to your stage

The challenge
A major established brand had no commercial reason to bet on an early-stage platform. Approaching them as a volume channel was a non-starter. The real obstacle was not the technology: it was that the platform had not yet earned the right to be in the procurement conversation. This is the positioning problem most AgTech founders face before their first enterprise or retail anchor client.
How it was solved
Reframed the value proposition entirely: not "reach more customers" but "reach a specific audience you currently cannot access without compromising your brand." Built a deal architecture that gave the partner control over how they were presented, removing the risk perception entirely.
Outcome
One lighthouse deal unlocked an entire category. The commercial framework became the template for signing a full portfolio of premium brands that had previously refused to engage. One credibility anchor changed every subsequent conversation.
The challenge
A capital-intensive, multi-year commitment to a government agency that had never purchased this technology category before. No internal procurement framework existed. No internal champion had seen it deployed at scale. This is the commercial reality for AgTech founders pursuing USDA contracts, state agricultural agencies, or international development institutions. The technology is sound. The buyer does not yet know how to say yes.
How it was solved
Designed a phased commitment structure that moved the buyer from risk-off to conviction in stages: technical validation, pilot economics, then full deployment contract. Built a custom ROI narrative around the agency's actual KPIs rather than commercial metrics: food security output, local production capacity, employment creation.
Outcome
The deal framework became the foundation for 13 subsequent enterprise deployments across 6 countries. Documented, not tribal: it survived team changes and market shifts because the sales process was built as a system, not carried in someone's head.
The challenge
Agricultural producers (livestock operators, growers, smallholders) have no digital footprint, do not attend tech conferences, and cannot be reached through conventional B2B outbound. Standard lead generation tools return nothing useful. This is the situation most AgTech founders face when their end user is a producer rather than a corporate buyer. The technology works in the field. Getting it in front of the right operators at scale is the unsolved problem.
How it was solved
Built a channel from scratch using organic content targeted precisely at the audience's actual behaviour and information habits. Designed onboarding around community trust signals rather than feature demonstrations. The content engine became the lead generation engine: prospects self-qualified before any human contact was made.
Outcome
7,000 active livestock producers across 11 countries within 6 months, at zero paid acquisition cost. A system that finds, qualifies, and converts agricultural producers continuously, without the founder running every outreach. The same engine logic applies to any AgTech company selling to producers that conventional channels cannot reach.
